Cohort launch gate

A cohort does not begin with anonymous responsibility.

Before any active cohort starts, this page and the family cohort brief must identify the accountable people.

Founder profiles

Approved biography, relevant experience, role, and direct contact path for each active founder.

Mentor roster

Name, program role, relevant experience, screening status, conduct acknowledgement, and training completion.

Safeguarding ownership

A named adult-oversight and safeguarding contact, escalation coverage, and the limits of student-led operations.

How accountability works

One owner for every workstream.

Program operations

Scheduling, attendance, family communication, and admissions response commitments require a named owner in the cohort brief.

Curriculum and mentorship

Each level requires a curriculum owner, active mentor roster, review expectations, and clear progression rules.

Safety and privacy

Reports, incidents, privacy requests, and data deletion need named responders and a documented handoff path.

Organizational transparency

Current status

Compute Forward’s formal organizational status, funding sources, operating budget, and finalized adult-oversight structure have not been provided in the project records. These items must be published or directly disclosed to families before an active cohort begins.

This transparency statement avoids implying nonprofit status, institutional affiliation, background checks, or adult supervision that has not been documented.
