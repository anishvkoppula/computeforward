## What Compute Forward Is

Compute Forward is a **free, student-led computer science education organization** designed to take students from zero programming experience to competition-ready skill. It operates as a **structured pipeline**, not a collection of random tutorials. The program is built around cohort-based learning, rigorous curriculum design, and measurable student outcomes.

**The core problem it solves:** most students who want to learn CS are stuck navigating disjointed YouTube videos, disconnected courses, and no accountability. Compute Forward replaces that with **structure, community, and real progression.**

---

## Core Principles

- **Free and accessible.** No paywalls, no gatekeeping. Any motivated student can apply regardless of background or prior experience.
- **Student-led.** Founded and run by high school students. Mentors are advanced competitive programmers who have gone through the same journey.
- **Outcome-driven.** The program tracks USACO promotions, problems solved, and project completions. Results, not just participation, are the measure.
- **Cohort-based.** Students progress together in small groups with peer accountability, live workshops, and 1-on-1 mentorship.
- **Competition-grounded.** Curriculum is reverse-engineered from USACO Bronze through Gold. Every concept maps to real competition problems, not toy exercises.

## Programs and Curriculum

The program has **four sequential levels** that form one unified pipeline. Each level builds directly on the previous one. Public level outlines and placement guidance are available; cohort-specific schedules and curriculum-owner-approved weekly syllabi must be published before enrollment.

### Level 1 — Python Foundations
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Target:** Absolute beginners, no prior experience required
- **Goal:** Master Python with a focus on computational thinking and build real projects

### Level 2 — Applied AI and Machine Learning
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Target:** Students who have completed Level 1 or have baseline Python proficiency
- **Goal:** Move from knowing Python to building with AI. Students work hands-on with core ML libraries, train real models, and complete applied mini-projects using tools like NumPy, pandas, scikit-learn, and an introduction to neural networks via TensorFlow or PyTorch

### Level 3 — Algorithms and Competitive Programming
- **Duration:** 10 weeks
- **Target:** Intermediate to advanced students, USACO Bronze through Silver prep
- **Goal:** Master core data structures, algorithmic patterns, and competitive programming strategy through structured problem solving and mock contests

### Level 4 — Passion Project and Research
- **Duration:** Ongoing, mentor-guided
- **Target:** Students who have completed prior levels or demonstrate strong independent readiness
- **Goal:** Build something real. Students develop an independent project — an AI application, a research paper, a startup prototype, or a technical tool — with direct mentorship from Compute Forward leadership. Output is portfolio-grade and meant to represent the student's best work

## Admissions and Application Process

- Interest applications are currently accepted on a rolling basis; an active cohort is not implied until exact logistics are published
- **Level 1 requires no prior experience**
- **Levels 2, 3, and 4** expect prior knowledge consistent with completing the preceding level
- Students apply by **selecting their level and submitting contact information**
- The admissions response **target is within 48 hours** of application submission
- The program is **free at every stage, permanently**
- Applicants under 18 require parent or guardian authorization; a parent or legal guardian submits for applicants under 13
- Exact cohort dates, schedule, workload, capacity, mentor roster, and safeguarding contacts are disclosed before an offer must be accepted

## Who It's For

Compute Forward is built for students who want more than a tutorial. The target student:

- Is self-motivated and wants structured progression
- Is a beginner who wants a real on-ramp into CS, or an intermediate student aiming for competitive programming
- Does not have access to expensive private tutoring or enrichment programs
- Wants community and accountability, not just content

---

## Positioning

Compute Forward is an **all-in-one computer science education organization for K–12 students.** It is **not** a tutoring service, **not** a single workshop, and **not** a course platform. It is a structured, multi-level program with a defined curriculum pipeline, application-based enrollment, cohort accountability, and measurable outcomes.

Think of it as the **free, student-built alternative to expensive CS enrichment programs**, grounded in the rigor of competitive programming.
